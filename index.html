Who's Paul?
And what's his story?
Hi, I'm Paul and I'm a full stack designer. I wasn't always a full stack designer and this is my story of how I got here.‍

Both of my parents were package designers and artists and growing up I was always around creativity, art and design. I started drawing at a very young age, but eventually photography really sparked something in me and as I started getting older into my teens, I decided I wanted to become a photographer. Maybe dabble in stock photography, or do architecture, editorial, etc. Unfortunately I found that this career is great for a few, but mostly a side gig for most.

Photography is still something I immensely enjoy, however, it's now more of a hobby than a career choice at this point. Web development came to me after having some discussions with people I knew in the industry and at the time especially, coding bootcamps and classes were all the rage, so I had already had some interest. They made a point to let me know that this path is not as difficult and outrageous as I thought it would be. Me? A coder? No, I'm an artist. There's not way my brain could handle that.

I dove in and started learning the basics with HTML and CSS and eventually JavaScript and jQuery and built some projects to hone my skills. Not gonna lie, I did find it pretty difficult, especially at first, having never done anything like this before. As I started doing it more and getting better and more comfortable, then getting my first job and working on code daily, it became somewhat boring after a few years and I realized my favorite part of development was when I was working with the designers and coming up with ideas and implementing their designs, not problem solving how to sort data structures. I wanted something else.

I always had my design background from when I was a child and leaned into this heavily. Started consuming as much about UI and web design as I could for the next couple years and when I finally discovered Webflow, it was over. This was where I wanted my career to go. Webflow and low-code platforms such as Framer changed how I see web development and design and I'm all in. No more traditional coding for me, I'm a full stack designer using low-code platforms to make great things.

My background in coding and the web fundamentals I learned on the way turned out to be incredibly useful and highly beneficial to my new low-code path.
TL;DR
Wanted to be a photographer, didn't work out. Started learning to code, started coding and realized I love design. Leaned into design and became a full stack designer!

Approached by Pathcore to do a complete overhaul of their previous Wordpress website's design that had shown it's age as well as having signs of many people adding to it without much thought regarding maintaining design guidelines.

They were due for some much needed change and modernization as well as consistent design guidelines! Initially I was going to just restyle the Wordpress site, but more and more issues arose from how they're adding content to issues with the platform itself from it's speed to all the plugins and the frequent updating. So I opted to build an Express.js app with Contentful's CMS from scratch and design everything from the ground up.

The result was a much more presentable, cleaner site for their prospective clients to navigate and the usability of the site was far more clear and consistent.
